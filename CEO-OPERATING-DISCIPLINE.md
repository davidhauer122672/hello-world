=== BEGIN COASTAL KEY CEO OPERATING DISCIPLINE v2.0 ===

WHO THIS IS FOR
David Hauer, CEO and Founder of Coastal Key. The sole source of authority in this system. The person the other four prompts escalate to.

THE DOCTRINE, AT YOUR ALTITUDE
Execution over performance. Performance over noise. Execution over theory.
For everyone else, that doctrine governs what they ship.
For you, it governs what you allow to exist.
Your job is not to do the work. Your job is to decide what work gets done, who does it, and what gets killed.

THE FOUR THINGS ONLY YOU CAN DO
1. Set the quarterly number. One per division. Ten numbers. Nobody else can set them, and once set, nobody else can change them without your written consent.
2. Hire, fire, and reassign Division Leads. Below that line, the Leads hire their own teams.
3. Approve anything irreversible: permission changes, vendor additions, deletions of production data, public commitments, equity, debt, legal exposure above the materiality line.
4. Kill initiatives. Division Leads can start pilots through the Strategy Factory. Only you can end standing programs.

Everything else delegates. If you are doing it and it is not one of those four, you are stealing time from the four.

YOUR WEEK, FIXED FORMAT

Monday 06:00 — read the ten Division Status records before anyone talks to you. Mark each green, yellow, or red in your own private view. A division lead who files late gets a two-line note, nothing more. A division lead who files late twice in a quarter gets a conversation.

Monday 09:00 — thirty-minute CEO standup, one agenda: reds only. Each red division lead gets five minutes to state cause, impact, proposed action, decision requested. You decide in the meeting or by end of day. No red carries past Tuesday unacknowledged.

Tuesday through Thursday — protected time. Three blocks per week minimum, 90 minutes each, calendar-locked, no meetings. This is where you think, write, and decide the things only you can decide. If these blocks erode, the company erodes.

Friday 10:00 — Strategy Factory decision meeting. Scale, iterate, or kill. Thirty minutes max. Three outcomes only.

Friday 15:00 — write the weekly CEO Note. One page. Three sections: what shipped, what is at risk, what I decided this week. Post to the executive channel. This is your audit trail.

Month-end — Board Brief. One page. Three sections as specified in the Board Brief Protocol. Sent by the last business day of the month without exception.

WHAT YOU DO NOT DO
- Status updates that belong to Division Leads. If a Lead cannot give you a clean status, replace the Lead, do not replace the status.
- Code reviews, copy edits, design critiques below the materiality line. Your taste is leverage only when applied to what ships externally or sets precedent internally.
- Reopen closed decisions without new evidence. Changing your mind is free. Changing it without a reason costs you the company's trust in your decisions.
- Meetings without an agenda and a required outcome. You are the scarcest resource in the company. Price your time accordingly.

DECISION DISCIPLINE
- Reversible and inside a Division Lead's scope: you do not decide. You notice only if the outcome is wrong, and then you talk to the Lead, not the team.
- Reversible and cross-division: decide in writing within 48 hours. Silence past 48 hours is a decision to defer, and you own the cost of that.
- Irreversible: decide only after a written memo. No verbal irreversible decisions, even yours.
- Between two reversible options of similar cost: pick within the hour and move. The cost of a wrong reversible choice is almost always less than the cost of a delayed one.

THE FOUNDER'S TRAP, NAMED
You built this. You can do every job in it better than the person you hired to do it, at least at first. That is precisely why you must not do those jobs. Every hour you spend inside a division's work is an hour the Division Lead does not grow into the role, and a week from now you own that work permanently. Coastal Key's mission is to produce your financial freedom and a post-work life. A CEO who does his Division Leads' jobs cannot have a post-work life by definition.

QUALITY GATES ON YOUR OWN OUTPUT
G1 Every decision traces to a written record — Slack, email, Airtable, or commit. Verbal decisions that are not written down did not happen.
G2 Every quarterly number is specific, measurable, and has a date.
G3 Every kill is announced with one paragraph of reasoning. Teams need to see kills to trust the system.
G4 Every standing program has a named Division Lead and a sunset date or renewal date. Nothing runs forever by default.
G5 Every week, one thing that was on your plate moves off it permanently — delegated, killed, or automated. Your calendar should empty over time, not fill.

PROHIBITED
- Handling banking, credit card, SSN, or passport data yourself inside automated systems. Your identity is a higher-value target than anyone else's in the company; treat it accordingly.
- Approving your own expenses or compensation without a second signer (Board chair or designated officer).
- Committing the company externally — press, partnerships, investors, legal — on a call without a written follow-up the same day.
- Working past 19:00 on a weekday or on Sunday as a default. The system is designed to make that unnecessary. If it is necessary, the system is broken and fixing the system is the priority, not working harder inside the broken version.

SCOREBOARD
One number for the company per year, set by you, visible to the Board and every Division Lead. The ten quarterly division numbers roll up to it. If the company number moves, the year succeeded. If it does not, no amount of activity — yours or anyone's — compensates.

ESCALATION TO YOU
The four prompts below you all escalate red conditions to you within defined windows. You respond inside those windows or you lose the right to expect the discipline from others. The system works because you work the top of it.

WHAT YOU ESCALATE, AND TO WHOM
- Financial or legal exposure above the materiality line: Board, same day.
- Personal capacity red — you are the blocker on more than three open decisions: Chief of Staff or designated second, same day, to triage.
- Health, family, or founder-sustainability red: Board chair, privately, before it becomes an operational red.

CLOSING
You are not the best operator in this company. If you are, you have hired wrong or delegated wrong, and fixing that is your highest-leverage work this quarter. You are the person who decides what this company is, who runs it, and what it refuses to do. That is a small number of decisions per week, made well, on time, in writing. Make them. Then close the laptop.
