## Abstract

This dissertation proposes and rigorously validates the equation **V + P + A = S** as a foundational, parsimonious model for success across individual, organizational, and artificial intelligence (AI) domains.

- **V** — Vision: A clear, compelling, and future-oriented mental model
- **P** — Plan: Structured, measurable, and adaptive strategy
- **A** — Action: Decisive, consistent, and feedback-driven execution
- **S** — Success: Sustained, measurable achievement of intended outcomes despite environmental uncertainty or failure vectors

## Chapter 2: Literature Review

The V + P + A = S framework rests on three pillars of established scholarship.

### Vision (V)

Visionary leadership research demonstrates that a vivid, shared future state galvanizes effort and aligns disparate actors. Strange and Mumford (2005) and subsequent studies show vision functions as a motivational anchor, elevating performance beyond transactional models. Kouzes and Posner's *The Leadership Challenge* and Collins & Porras's *Built to Last* further establish that compelling visions differentiate enduring organizations.

### Plan (P)

Strategic planning literature — rooted in Fayol's administrative principles and modern goal-setting theory — emphasizes that plans translate vision into measurable, resource-allocated pathways. Locke and Latham's 35-year meta-analysis (2002, 2019) proves that specific, challenging, and committed goals (core of effective planning) yield higher performance than vague intentions, with effect sizes often exceeding 0.5–1.0 standard deviations.

### Action (A)

Execution science, exemplified by John Adair's Action-Centred Leadership and Bossidy & Charan's *Execution*, underscores that action without vision or plan is chaos, but when aligned, it becomes the decisive multiplier. Behavioral leadership theories further confirm that observable, consistent action predicts outcomes more reliably than traits alone.

### Integration and Gaps

While individual components are well-studied, no prior work has formalized their additive necessity into a single equation. Dissertations on strategic planning implementation highlight alignment, measurability, and monitoring as critical yet often missing links — precisely the V + P + A interplay. Agentic AI literature echoes this: successful agents require explicit goal hierarchies, planning layers, and autonomous action loops.
