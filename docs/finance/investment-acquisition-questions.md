## II. Seller Discovery Questions

### A. Business Overview

1. How many properties are currently under management? Breakdown by type (single-family, condo, multi-family, commercial)?
2. What is your current monthly recurring revenue (MRR)? Annual revenue for the last 3 fiscal years?
3. What is your average management fee per property? Range (lowest to highest)?
4. What percentage of revenue is recurring vs. one-time (lease-up fees, maintenance markups)?
5. What is your client concentration? Top 5 clients as % of total revenue?
6. What is your annual client churn rate? Primary reasons for client loss?
7. How many properties have you onboarded in the last 12 months? Lost?

### B. Operations & Staffing

8. How many full-time employees? Part-time? Contractors?
9. What is your total annual labor cost (including benefits)?
10. What technology/software stack do you currently use? (Property management software, accounting, CRM)
11. What percentage of your operations is manual vs. automated?
12. Describe your inspection process. Frequency? Documentation standard?
13. Do you have documented Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)?
14. What is your average response time for maintenance requests? Emergency requests?
15. How do you handle after-hours emergencies?

### C. Financial Deep Dive

16. Provide P&L statements for the last 3 fiscal years.
17. What is your current EBITDA margin?
18. What are your top 5 operating expenses by dollar amount?
19. Do you have any outstanding debt, liens, or financial obligations?
20. What is your accounts receivable aging? Percentage over 60 days?
21. What are your vendor payment terms? Any outstanding vendor disputes?
22. Do you carry Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance? Coverage amount?

### D. Client Portfolio Quality

23. Provide a complete client roster with: property address, management fee, contract start date, contract term, renewal status.
24. What percentage of clients are on month-to-month vs. annual contracts?
25. What is the average property value in your portfolio?
26. What percentage of properties are seasonal/vacant vs. occupied year-round?
27. Are there any pending client complaints, disputes, or legal actions?
28. Do any clients have unusual contractual terms or side agreements?
29. What is your Net Promoter Score (NPS) or equivalent client satisfaction metric?

### E. Market & Competitive Position

30. Who are your top 3 competitors in your service area?
31. What differentiates your service from competitors?
32. What is your primary lead generation channel? (Referral, web, advertising, other)
33. What is your customer acquisition cost (CAC)?
34. Do you have any exclusive vendor relationships or territorial agreements?
35. Are there any regulatory or licensing requirements specific to your area?

### F. Seller Motivation & Terms

36. Why are you selling?
37. What is your asking price? How was it determined?
38. Are you willing to provide seller financing? Terms?
39. Will you agree to a non-compete clause? Duration and geographic scope?
40. Are you willing to assist with transition for 90 days post-close?
41. What is your desired timeline for closing?
42. Are there any other parties interested or offers pending?

## III. Post-LOI Due Diligence Checklist

### Legal & Compliance

- [ ] Corporate formation documents (Articles, Operating Agreement)
- [ ] All management contracts (current and recently terminated)
- [ ] Vendor contracts and agreements
- [ ] Insurance policies (GL, WC, E&O, Cyber, Property)
- [ ] License and permit verification
- [ ] Litigation history search (state + federal)
- [ ] Tax returns (3 years, corporate and personal if sole proprietor)
- [ ] Employee/contractor agreements and classifications
- [ ] Non-compete and non-solicitation agreements

### Financial Verification

- [ ] Bank statements (12 months minimum)
- [ ] Reconciled P&L vs. bank deposits
- [ ] Trust account audit (if applicable)
- [ ] Accounts receivable aging report
- [ ] Accounts payable aging report
- [ ] Payroll records and tax filings
- [ ] Capital expenditure history (3 years)
- [ ] Revenue by property (trailing 12 months)

### Operational Assessment

- [ ] Property inspection records (sample 20% of portfolio)
- [ ] Client satisfaction data (surveys, reviews, NPS)
- [ ] Employee interviews (key personnel)
- [ ] Technology stack evaluation and migration plan
- [ ] Vendor relationship assessment (top 10 vendors)
- [ ] Outstanding maintenance requests
- [ ] Emergency response history and outcomes

### Integration Planning

- [ ] Client communication plan (Day 1 letter)
- [ ] Staff retention/termination decisions
- [ ] Technology migration timeline (to Coastal Key platform)
- [ ] Vendor renegotiation opportunities
- [ ] Brand transition strategy
- [ ] 90-day integration playbook

## IV. Valuation Framework

### Preferred Methodology: EBITDA Multiple

| EBITDA Multiple Range | Scenario | Conditions |
|----------------------|----------|------------|
| 3.0-4.0x | Distressed / Motivated | High churn, aging owner, no systems |
| 4.0-5.0x | Fair Market | Stable book, moderate growth, some systems |
| 5.0-6.0x | Premium | Low churn, strong growth, good systems, clean books |
| 6.0x+ | Strategic | Unique territory, exclusive contracts, synergies |

### Alternative Metrics

| Metric | Multiplier | Use Case |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Revenue Multiple | 1.0-2.5x annual revenue | Quick screening |
| Per-Door Value | $1,500-$3,500 per door | Industry benchmark |
| MRR Multiple | 24-36x monthly recurring | SaaS-style valuation |

### Coastal Key Acquisition Premium/Discount Factors

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Automation opportunity (manual → AI) | +15-25% value through margin expansion |
| Client retention risk during transition | -10-15% haircut |
| Geographic density with existing portfolio | +10% strategic premium |
| Seller financing available | +5% (reduces capital at risk) |
| Non-compete included | Required (no deal without it) |

## V. Deal Structure Preferences

### Preferred Terms

| Parameter | Preference |
|-----------|-----------|
| Purchase Price | 4-5x EBITDA |
| Payment Structure | 60% at close, 20% at 6 months (earnout), 20% at 12 months (earnout) |
| Earnout Conditions | Client retention >= 85% at measurement date |
| Non-Compete | 3 years, 50-mile radius |
| Transition Assistance | 90 days, seller available 10 hours/week |
| Seller Financing | Preferred, 5-year term, 6-8% interest |
| Working Capital | Included in purchase price (minimum 30 days operating cash) |
