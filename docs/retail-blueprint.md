## 1. Brand Identity System

### Brand Positioning
- **Category**: Premium-accessible athletic and lifestyle footwear + apparel
- **Price Point**: Mid-to-premium ($65–$220 footwear, $25–$120 apparel)
- **Target**: Style-conscious consumers aged 18–45 who value quality over quantity
- **Positioning Statement**: Curated, not cluttered. Every item on the floor was chosen, not just ordered.

### Brand Voice
- **Tone**: Confident, knowledgeable, never desperate
- **Language**: Clean, direct, zero filler
- **Prohibited**: "Sale!" in all caps, exclamation-point-heavy copy, "limited time only" pressure tactics
- **Guiding Principle**: Sell through expertise, not through discount

### Visual Identity
- **Primary Palette**: Charcoal (#2D2D2D), Warm White (#F5F0EB), Accent Gold (#C4A265)
- **Typography**: Clean sans-serif (Helvetica Neue or equivalent)
- **Store Aesthetic**: Minimal fixtures, warm lighting, gallery-style product display
- **Packaging**: Matte black bags with embossed logo, tissue paper wrap standard

## 2. SKU Strategy & Inventory Architecture

### Product Mix (Ferrari Standard: every SKU earns its shelf space)

| Category | Mix % | Avg. Retail | Avg. Margin | Turn Target |
|----------|-------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Core Footwear** (classics, staples) | 35% | $120 | 50% | 4x/year |
| **Trend Footwear** (seasonal, limited) | 15% | $160 | 55% | 6x/year |
| **Core Apparel** (tees, hoodies, joggers) | 25% | $55 | 55% | 5x/year |
| **Trend Apparel** (collabs, seasonal) | 10% | $85 | 60% | 6x/year |
| **Accessories** (socks, hats, bags, laces) | 10% | $25 | 65% | 8x/year |
| **Impulse / Counter** (care kits, pins, stickers) | 5% | $12 | 70% | 10x/year |

### Inventory Rules
- **Opening inventory**: $45,000 at cost (~$90,000 at retail)
- **Reorder point**: When SKU hits 30% of initial stock
- **Dead stock rule**: Any SKU with zero sales in 60 days gets marked down 25%. No sales in 90 days = liquidate at cost.
- **Size curve**: Stock to demand curve, not even distribution. 60% of units in sizes 8–11 (men's) / 7–9 (women's)
- **New arrivals cadence**: Minimum 1 new drop every 2 weeks to drive repeat visits

### SKU Count Targets
| Category | SKU Count | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Footwear | 80–100 styles | 3–5 size runs each |
| Apparel | 60–80 styles | S/M/L/XL, limited XXL |
| Accessories | 40–50 items | Counter and wall display |
| **Total** | **180–230 SKUs** | Curated, not overwhelming |

```
│                                               │
│  ┌───────────────────────────────────────┐   │
│  │           BACK STOCK / OFFICE          │   │
│  │      (High-margin reserve + POS)       │   │
│  └───────────────────────────────────────┘   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Zone Strategy (Behavioral Psychology)

| Zone | Sq Ft | Purpose | Psychology |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Entry / Feature Wall** | 150 | New arrivals, hero products | First impression = brand statement. Rotate biweekly. |
| **Right Wall (Footwear)** | 300 | Core + trend footwear display | 90% of customers turn right upon entry. Best product = right wall. |
| **Center Floor (Apparel)** | 350 | Racks, folding tables, mannequins | Browsing zone. Reduce density — 1 rack per 50 sq ft max. |
| **Left Wall (Impulse)** | 100 | Accessories, counter display, checkout | Capture add-on sales at decision point. Avg +$18/transaction. |
| **Try-On Area** | 150 | Seating, mirrors, shoe fitting | Comfort = conversion. If they sit down, close rate jumps 60%. |
| **Back Stock / Office** | 150 | Inventory reserve, admin | Not customer-facing. Maximize sellable floor space. |

## 5. Omnichannel Integration (POS + E-commerce)

### Technology Stack

| System | Provider | Purpose |
|--------|----------|---------|
| POS | Shopify POS | In-store transactions, inventory sync |
| E-commerce | Shopify | Online store, same inventory pool |
| Inventory | Shopify Inventory | Single source of truth for all channels |
| Email | Klaviyo | Post-purchase flows, abandon cart, VIP segments |
| Social | Instagram Shopping + TikTok Shop | Shoppable posts, direct checkout |
| Analytics | Shopify Analytics + Google Analytics 4 | Revenue attribution, traffic source, conversion |

### Sync Rules
- **Inventory**: Real-time sync between POS and online. One pool. No separate counts.
- **Pricing**: Identical online and in-store. No channel-specific discounting.
- **Orders**: Buy Online, Pick Up In Store (BOPIS) enabled from Day 1
- **Returns**: Accept online returns in-store and vice versa
- **Customer profiles**: Unified across channels. Purchase history follows the customer, not the channel.

## 6. Launch Plan

### Soft Launch (Weeks 1–2)
- **Purpose**: Test systems, train staff, gather feedback
- **Hours**: Reduced (11 AM – 6 PM, 5 days/week)
- **Inventory**: 70% of opening stock on floor
- **Marketing**: Friends & family only, private Instagram stories
- **Metrics to track**: POS reliability, transaction speed, customer flow patterns

### Hard Launch (Week 3)
- **Purpose**: Full public opening
- **Hours**: Full (10 AM – 8 PM Mon–Sat, 11 AM – 6 PM Sun)
- **Event**: Grand opening with local influencer presence, DJ, giveaways
- **Marketing push**:
  - Instagram/TikTok countdown (7 days pre-launch)
  - Local press release
  - Google My Business live with photos
  - Geo-targeted ads (5-mile radius, 18–45 demo)
- **Launch offers**: First 50 customers get exclusive tote bag (cost: $4/unit, perceived value: $25)
- **Budget**: $3,000 for launch week marketing blitz
