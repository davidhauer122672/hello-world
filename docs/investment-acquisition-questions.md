# Investment Acquisition Due Diligence — Elite Question Framework
**Version**: 1.0.0 | **Classification**: Investor-Grade | **Positioning**: Buy-Side Acquisition Analysis

> These 40 questions are designed to survive a PE firm interrogation. Each question exposes risk, validates value, or kills the deal before capital is deployed. No softballs.

---

## Section 1: Financial Integrity (Questions 1–10)

### Revenue & Cash Flow
1. **What is the trailing 12-month (TTM) revenue, and how does it compare to the prior 3 fiscal years?** Require audited financials. If unaudited, discount credibility by 30%.

2. **What percentage of revenue is recurring vs. one-time?** Recurring revenue >60% = strong. Below 40% = red flag. Identify contract terms, renewal rates, and churn.

3. **What is the gross margin by service line?** Break down by property management fees, maintenance, emergency response, and ancillary services. Margins below 35% on core services require explanation.

4. **Provide a 24-month cash flow statement with actual vs. projected.** Variance >15% in any quarter = weak forecasting discipline. Ask why.

5. **What are the top 5 clients by revenue concentration?** If any single client exceeds 20% of total revenue, the business has a dependency risk that must be priced into valuation.

6. **What is the current debt structure?** Detail all notes payable, lines of credit, SBA loans, equipment financing. Include interest rates, maturity dates, and covenant requirements.

7. **Are there any off-balance-sheet liabilities?** Pending litigation, personal guarantees, deferred maintenance obligations, unfunded pension commitments, or lease obligations not on the books.

8. **What is the owner's actual compensation (salary + distributions + perks + personal expenses run through the business)?** Recast earnings to determine true EBITDA. Most small businesses understate owner comp by 25–40%.

9. **What is the accounts receivable aging?** AR >90 days as a percentage of total AR. If >15%, the business has a collections problem. Ask about write-off history.

10. **What is the customer lifetime value (LTV) to customer acquisition cost (CAC) ratio?** LTV:CAC below 3:1 = unsustainable growth model. Above 5:1 = strong unit economics.

## Section 2: Operational Risk (Questions 11–18)

### Process & Dependency
11. **If the owner disappeared tomorrow, could the business operate for 90 days without revenue decline?** If no, the business is the owner. You're buying a job, not a company.

12. **Document every process that currently lives in the owner's head.** Verbal knowledge = zero enterprise value. If it isn't written down with SOPs, it doesn't exist as a transferable asset.

13. **What is the employee turnover rate for the last 3 years?** Turnover >25% annually in operations roles = culture problem or compensation problem. Both are expensive to fix.

14. **Are there any key-person dependencies beyond the owner?** Identify any employee whose departure would cause >10% revenue loss or operational disruption. Quantify the risk.

15. **What is the current insurance coverage?** General liability, E&O, workers' comp, umbrella, cyber, property. Identify any gaps. Underinsured businesses are ticking time bombs.

16. **What pending or historical legal actions exist?** Include settled cases (terms may restrict future operations), ongoing disputes, regulatory complaints, and any HOA/condo board conflicts.

17. **What is the technology stack and what would it cost to replace?** Legacy systems, proprietary software dependencies, vendor lock-in. If the business runs on spreadsheets and a phone, modernization cost must be priced in.

18. **What licenses, certifications, or regulatory requirements does the business hold, and are they transferable?** Non-transferable licenses can kill a deal at closing.

## Section 3: Market Position (Questions 19–26)

### Competitive Landscape & Moat
19. **What is the total addressable market (TAM) within the operating geography?** Number of residential units, commercial properties, HOA communities. Define the ceiling.

20. **What is current market share by property count and by revenue?** If market share <5%, the business is a participant, not a leader. Growth upside exists but so does vulnerability.

21. **Who are the top 3 competitors, and what do they do better?** Honest answer required. If the owner says "nobody," they either don't know their market or are lying. Both are disqualifying.

22. **What is the Net Promoter Score (NPS) or equivalent client satisfaction metric?** NPS >50 = strong. NPS <20 = retention risk. No measurement at all = flying blind.

23. **What is the client retention rate over 3 years?** Annual retention below 85% means the business is a leaking bucket. Acquisition can't outrun churn forever.

24. **What proprietary advantages does the business possess?** Exclusive vendor relationships, technology, geographic lock-in, brand reputation, regulatory barriers to entry. If the answer is "our service is better" — that's not a moat.

25. **What is the average contract duration and renewal process?** Month-to-month = maximum vulnerability. Multi-year with auto-renewal = defensible revenue.

26. **What geographic or demographic trends affect this market over the next 5 years?** Population growth, seasonal migration patterns, new construction, regulatory changes, insurance market shifts.

## Section 4: Scalability (Questions 27–34)

### Growth Mechanics
27. **What would it take to double revenue in 24 months without doubling headcount?** If the answer requires linear headcount growth, the business model doesn't scale. Technology and process leverage are required.

28. **What is the current capacity utilization?** Can existing infrastructure, staff, and systems handle 50% more volume? Identify the first bottleneck that breaks.

29. **What is the cost to acquire a new client (fully loaded)?** Include marketing, sales time, onboarding, first-month operational ramp. Compare to LTV (Question 10).

30. **Is there a repeatable sales process, or is growth dependent on owner relationships and referrals?** Referral-only growth = no scalable acquisition channel. Document the funnel.

31. **What adjacent services could be cross-sold to existing clients?** Identify revenue expansion opportunities: concierge, renovation management, insurance brokerage, investment advisory.

32. **What is the technology roadmap for the next 12 months?** If the answer is "none," the business will be outcompeted by tech-enabled operators within 3 years.

33. **Can the business operate in multiple geographies with the current model?** Single-market businesses have a ceiling. Multi-market expansion requires documented playbooks.

34. **What are the unit economics per property under management?** Revenue per property, cost per property, margin per property. This is the atomic unit of the business.

---

## Section 5: Exit Strategy & Valuation (Questions 35–40)

### Deal Structure & Terminal Value
35. **What is the seller's expected valuation, and what methodology supports it?** Multiples of EBITDA, SDE, or revenue. If the seller can't articulate methodology, they're guessing.

36. **What is the recast EBITDA after normalizing owner compensation, one-time expenses, and non-operating items?** This is the only number that matters for valuation. Everything else is narrative.

37. **What are the proposed deal terms?** Asset sale vs. stock sale, earnout structure, seller financing, non-compete terms, transition period, escrow holdback.

38. **What is the seller's motivation and timeline?** Retirement, burnout, health, partner dispute, market timing. Motivation drives negotiation leverage. Urgency = discount.

39. **What would a 12-month post-acquisition integration plan look like?** Identify Day 1, Day 30, Day 90, Day 180, Day 365 milestones. If the seller can't articulate transition steps, they haven't thought about it.

40. **What kills this deal?** Ask the seller directly. The honest answer reveals more than every other question combined. If they say "nothing," they're not being honest.

## Scoring Framework

| Section | Weight | Pass Threshold |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| Financial Integrity | 30% | 7/10 questions satisfactory |
| Operational Risk | 25% | 6/8 questions satisfactory |
| Market Position | 20% | 6/8 questions satisfactory |
| Scalability | 15% | 6/8 questions satisfactory |
| Exit Strategy | 10% | 4/6 questions satisfactory |

**Overall**: Score <70% = Walk. Score 70–85% = Negotiate hard. Score >85% = Move fast.
