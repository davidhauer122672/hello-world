# Investment Acquisition Due Diligence — Elite Question Framework
**Version**: 1.0.0 | **Classification**: Investor-Grade | **Positioning**: Buy-Side Acquisition Analysis

> 40 questions designed to survive a PE firm interrogation. Each exposes risk, validates value, or kills the deal.

## Section 1: Financial Integrity (1–10)
1. TTM revenue vs prior 3 fiscal years (require audited financials)
2. Recurring vs one-time revenue percentage (>60% recurring = strong)
3. Gross margin by service line (below 35% on core = explain)
4. 24-month cash flow actual vs projected (>15% variance = weak)
5. Top 5 clients by revenue concentration (>20% single client = risk)
6. Current debt structure (all notes, LOC, SBA, equipment)
7. Off-balance-sheet liabilities (litigation, guarantees, deferred maintenance)
8. Owner actual compensation recast (salary + distributions + perks)
9. Accounts receivable aging (>90 days >15% = collections problem)
10. LTV:CAC ratio (<3:1 unsustainable, >5:1 strong)

## Section 2: Operational Risk (11–18)
11. Can business operate 90 days without owner? (if no = buying a job)
12. Document every process in owner's head (verbal = zero enterprise value)
13. Employee turnover rate 3 years (>25% = culture/comp problem)
14. Key-person dependencies beyond owner (>10% revenue impact)
15. Insurance coverage gaps (GL, E&O, WC, umbrella, cyber)
16. Pending/historical legal actions
17. Technology stack replacement cost
18. License/certification transferability

## Section 3: Market Position (19–26)
19. TAM within operating geography
20. Market share by property count and revenue
21. Top 3 competitors and what they do better
22. NPS or client satisfaction metric (>50 strong, <20 risk)
23. Client retention rate over 3 years (<85% = leaking bucket)
24. Proprietary advantages / moat
25. Average contract duration and renewal process
26. Geographic/demographic trends next 5 years

## Section 4: Scalability (27–34)
27. What to double revenue in 24mo without doubling headcount?
28. Current capacity utilization / first bottleneck
29. Fully loaded client acquisition cost
30. Repeatable sales process vs owner-dependent referrals?
31. Adjacent cross-sell opportunities
32. Technology roadmap next 12 months
33. Multi-geography expansion capability
34. Unit economics per property under management

## Section 5: Exit Strategy (35–40)
35. Seller expected valuation + methodology
36. Recast EBITDA after normalization
37. Proposed deal terms (asset/stock, earnout, non-compete)
38. Seller motivation and timeline
39. 12-month post-acquisition integration plan
40. What kills this deal? (honest answer required)

## Scoring: <70% Walk | 70-85% Negotiate Hard | >85% Move Fast
