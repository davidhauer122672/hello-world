St. Lucie County.

Overview

St. Lucie County is the fastest-growing corridor on the Treasure Coast. Port St. Lucie, Fort Pierce, and Hutchinson Island represent an expanding inventory of seasonal and investment properties that require consistent, professional oversight.

Communities served

Port St. Lucie

The largest city in St. Lucie County by population, Port St. Lucie includes master-planned communities, golf course properties, and waterfront homes along the St. Lucie River and its tributaries. Many homeowners split time between Port St. Lucie and northern residences, leaving properties unoccupied for four to seven months each year. We service homes in Tradition, St. Lucie West, PGA Village, Tesoro, and all Port St. Lucie neighborhoods.

Fort Pierce

The county seat, Fort Pierce offers a mix of historic downtown properties, waterfront homes on the Indian River Lagoon, and newer developments on the western corridor. Fort Pierce properties often include older construction with systems that require closer monitoring -- galvanized plumbing, aging electrical panels, and window-unit air conditioning that has been supplemented with central systems.

Hutchinson Island

A barrier island stretching through both Martin and St. Lucie counties, the St. Lucie portion of Hutchinson Island is home to oceanfront condominiums, beach houses, and resort-style communities. Island properties face elevated salt air exposure, hurricane risk, and strict building code requirements. Our inspections account for the accelerated wear that coastal properties experience.

Response times

St. Lucie County is our secondary operating zone, positioned directly north of our Martin County headquarters. We maintain active inspection routes throughout the county every week.

  • Routine inspections: Scheduled visits follow the full 47-checkpoint Sentinel Standard on every visit. Biweekly, weekly, or custom cadence as contracted.
  • Same-day response: Non-emergency service requests received before 10 AM are addressed the same business day.
  • Emergency response: Critical issues -- active water intrusion, HVAC failure, security breach, storm damage -- receive on-site response within four hours, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.
  • Storm response: Pre-storm preparation begins 72 hours before projected landfall. Post-storm inspections begin within 18 hours of conditions permitting safe access.
