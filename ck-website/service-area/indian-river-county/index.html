Indian River County.

Overview

Indian River County is the northern boundary of our Treasure Coast coverage area. Vero Beach, Sebastian, and Indian River Shores are established communities with high concentrations of seasonal residents, barrier island estates, and waterfront properties that require year-round professional oversight.

Communities served

Vero Beach

The cultural and commercial center of Indian River County, Vero Beach is home to barrier island estates, historic beachside neighborhoods, and mainland communities stretching west to the citrus groves. Vero Beach attracts a significant seasonal population, with many homeowners dividing time between Florida and northeastern residences. Properties range from oceanfront condominiums to multi-acre mainland estates with pools, guest houses, and extensive landscaping.

Sebastian

A riverfront community on the Indian River Lagoon, Sebastian offers a mix of waterfront homes, canal properties, and residential neighborhoods. Many Sebastian homeowners are seasonal residents or investors who require regular property monitoring. The proximity to Sebastian Inlet and the lagoon means properties face salt air exposure and heightened storm vulnerability during hurricane season.

Indian River Shores

An exclusive barrier island municipality between Vero Beach and the unincorporated beach communities to the north. Indian River Shores properties are predominantly high-value oceanfront and riverfront estates. The community maintains its own public safety department, and our inspectors coordinate with local law enforcement and HOA security when accessing gated properties.

Response times

Indian River County is our northern operating zone. We maintain scheduled inspection routes in the county every week and adjust route density based on seasonal client concentration.

  • Routine inspections: Scheduled visits follow the full 47-checkpoint Sentinel Standard on every visit. Biweekly, weekly, or custom cadence as contracted.
  • Same-day response: Non-emergency service requests received before 10 AM are addressed the same business day.
  • Emergency response: Critical issues -- active water intrusion, HVAC failure, security breach, storm damage -- receive on-site response within four hours, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.
  • Storm response: Pre-storm preparation begins 72 hours before projected landfall. Post-storm inspections begin within 18 hours of conditions permitting safe access.
