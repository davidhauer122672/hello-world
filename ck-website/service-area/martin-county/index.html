Service Area

Martin County.

Martin County is home to our headquarters and the densest concentration of seasonal estates on the Treasure Coast. We operate here daily and maintain the shortest response times in our coverage area.

Communities served

Stuart

The county seat and our base of operations. Stuart properties include downtown historic homes, waterfront residences along the St. Lucie River, and gated communities throughout the greater Stuart area. We service single-family homes, townhomes, and condominiums across all Stuart zip codes.

Sewall's Point

A peninsula community surrounded by the Indian River Lagoon and the St. Lucie River. Sewall's Point is home to high-value waterfront estates with complex systems including seawalls, docks, boat lifts, and pool enclosures. Many properties are occupied only during the winter season, making professional home watch essential.

Jupiter Island

One of the most exclusive barrier island communities in the United States. Jupiter Island properties require discreet, security-conscious service with strict access protocols. We coordinate with private security firms, estate managers, and HOA gatekeepers to ensure seamless access on every visit.

Hobe Sound

A quiet coastal community with a mix of oceanfront condominiums, golf course estates, and family homes. Hobe Sound properties benefit from our proximity and our relationships with local vendors who service the area regularly.

Palm City

A residential community west of Stuart known for equestrian properties, larger lots, and newer planned developments. Palm City properties often include outbuildings, barns, well systems, and septic infrastructure that require specialized inspection knowledge.

Response times

Martin County is our primary operating zone. Response times reflect our physical proximity and the density of our inspection routes in the area.

  • Routine inspections: Scheduled visits follow the Sentinel Standard with no variation. Biweekly, weekly, or custom cadence as contracted.
  • Same-day response: All non-emergency service requests received before noon are addressed the same business day.
  • Emergency response: Critical issues -- active water intrusion, HVAC failure, security breach, storm damage -- receive on-site response within two hours, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.
  • Storm response: Pre-storm preparation begins 72 hours before projected landfall. Post-storm inspections begin within 12 hours of conditions permitting safe access.
